Article 1: How to Set Up Your Tiny Apartment

Posted on Sept 12, 2025

: How to Set Up Your Tiny Apartment

Welcome to your new apartment—also known as “the place where your money goes to die.” Setting up a tiny space doesn’t have to be stressful, though. Start with the basics: somewhere to sleep, somewhere to sit, and somewhere to study (yes, your bed does not count as a desk, even if you believe in yourself). Keep things minimal because your landlord definitely measured the room with a ruler from Dollarama.

Storage will be your best friend. Think vertical shelves, under‑bed bins, and those magical command hooks that save your security deposit. Lighting matters too—fluorescent overhead lights give “interrogation room,” not “vibes,” so grab a warm lamp or two. And don’t forget a small cleaning kit: broom, all‑purpose spray, and the courage to face whatever is under the fridge.

Make the space yours with posters, pillows, or a plant you may or may not accidentally kill by November. Overall, aim for cozy, functional, and not a fire hazard.

Article 2 (Step‑by‑Step): How to Not Go Broke in Your First Month

Posted on Sept 19, 2025

(Step‑by‑Step): How to Not Go Broke in Your First Month

Let’s be honest: university is basically a financial survival game. Here’s the step‑by‑step guide to keeping your wallet alive.

Steps:

  1. Track your spending. Not in your head. You will lie to yourself. Use an app.
  2. Make a weekly budget. Something realistic—like $40 for groceries, not $4.
  3. Cook at home. Uber Eats is the final boss of going broke.
  4. Use your student discounts. If a place offers 10% off, you say “thank you.”
  5. Avoid impulse buys. Do you need that Laurier hoodie? No. Will you buy it anyway? Probably.
  6. Plan for surprise expenses. Midterm printing, random club fees, replacing the mug you dropped—budget for it.

If you follow even half of these steps, congrats—you’re already doing better than most freshmen.

Article 3: How to Make Friends Without Being Weird

Posted on Oct 1, 2025

: How to Make Friends Without Being Weird

Making friends in university can feel like speed‑dating but with more group projects. Good news: everyone around you is just as nervous and pretending they aren’t. Start simple—talk to the people sitting near you in lectures. A quick “hey, did you get the assignment?” is basically the academic version of “what’s your sign?”

Join clubs that actually interest you. If you force yourself into Chess Club but you hate chess, you will simply suffer. Social events, residence activities, and study groups are all easy ways to meet people without feeling like you’re crash‑landing into a conversation.

And the secret tip? Be genuinely interested. People love talking about themselves. Ask questions, laugh at their jokes (even if they’re mid), and you’re golden. Friendships build slowly—don’t panic if you’re not in a squad by Week 2.
